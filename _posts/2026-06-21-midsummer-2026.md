Midsummer is a major national holiday here in Sweden. It's well-known for its pagan vibes.

I wanted to play into this and do a bit of casual holiday magick. I have always had good results with holiday magick, and midsummer is very prominent in the collective psyche where I live. Holidays in general seem to present rich egregores with power to tap into, at least those that are revered within the practioner's cultural context. Not an opportunity to be passed lightly.

What I came up with was a ritual where I would honour and ask favours of Freyr and Freya, twin god and goddess of midsummer in Nordic (neo-)paganism, culminating in the offering and destruction of clay idols made in their likeness, through drenching in water. The culmination was inspired by an entirely different spiritual tradition: Ganesh chaturthi, which terminates with the drenching and dissolution of clay statues of Ganesh in water.

## The Idols
![Clay icdols of Freyr and Freya](https://inkomstkatt.github.io/Diaries-of-a-rave-magician/assets/img/20260618_freyrfreya.webp)

I spent about a total of 20 work hours creating two air-drying clay idols of Freyr and Freya over several evenings starting two weeks before midsummer. I did not have much previous experience working with clay or indeed sculpting at all, but found it similar enough to previously known crafting materials and hobby skills that it felt easy to get into. In the end I was quite happy with the results. Worthy devotional objects, and offerings, indeed.

I stuck to an abstract kind of modern style that incorporated elements of known archeological depictions of the gods (of which there were surprisingly few, as it turned out). The gods bore exaggerated sexual characteristics. These are pagan fertility deities, after all. Freyr had a cone helmet and a beard, based on known historical depictions. Freya meanwhile lacked much historical reference material and her depiction with long flowing hair was based on descriptions and later depictions instead. Furthermore they each displayed a rune associated with them. For Freyr this is ingwaz, for Freya fehu.

They were about 7-8 cms tall, 4-5 cms wide, and 5-6 cms deep. On midsummer eve they had been left to dry in room temperature for 24 hours since the last work was finished.

## What the Gods Foretold
![What the gods foretold of the coming year](https://inkomstkatt.github.io/Diaries-of-a-rave-magician/assets/img/20260619_2356032.webp)

### Freya’s Divination
1. Ansuz – The rune of the Aesir. Wisdom, communication. Spirit, life force, and inspiration.
2. Tiwaz – Forward and outward flowing energy. Boldness with balance. Masculinity. The warrior rune of Tyr.
3. Ehwaz – The horse rune. Travel and communication between different planes. A shamanic power rune.

### Freyr’s Divination:
1. Sowilo – The life-giving power of the sun. Summer. Warmth and light.
2. Jera – The year's growth. Sowing in order to reap. Darkness meeting the light. Spring. The cycle of life.
3. Aegir – The moose rune. The animal kingdom. Inner strength. Survival instinct. Protection against evil forces.
