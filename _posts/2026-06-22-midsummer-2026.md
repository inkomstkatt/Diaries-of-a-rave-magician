Midsummer is a major national holiday here in Sweden. It's well-known for its pagan vibes.

I wanted to play into this and do a bit of holiday magick. I have always had good results with holiday magick. Holidays in general seem to present rich sources of power to tap into, and midsummer in particular is very prominent in the collective psyche where I live. Not an opportunity to be passed lightly.

What I came up with was a ritual where I would honour and ask favours of Freyr and Freya, twin god and goddess of midsummer in Nordic (neo-)paganism, culminating in the offering and destruction of clay idols made in their likeness through drenching in water. The culmination was inspired by an entirely different spiritual tradition: Ganesh chaturthi, which terminates with the drenching and dissolution of clay statues of Ganesh in water.

## The Idols
![Clay idols of Freyr and Freya](https://inkomstkatt.github.io/Diaries-of-a-rave-magician/assets/img/20260618_freyrfreya.webp)

I spent a total of about 20 work hours creating two air-drying clay idols of Freyr and Freya over several evenings starting two weeks before midsummer. I did not have much previous experience working with clay or indeed sculpting at all, but found it similar enough to previously known crafting materials and hobby skills that it felt easy to get into. In the end I was quite happy with the results. Worthy devotional objects, and offerings, indeed.

I stuck to an abstract kind of modern style that incorporated elements of known archeological depictions of the gods (of which there were surprisingly few, as it turned out). The gods bore exaggerated sexual characteristics. These are pagan fertility deities, after all. Freyr had a cone helmet and a beard, based on known historical depictions. Freya meanwhile lacked much historical reference material and her depiction with long flowing hair was based on descriptions and later modern artwork. Furthermore they each displayed a rune associated with them. For Freyr this was ingwaz, for Freya fehu.

They were about 8 cms tall, 5 cms wide, and 6 cms deep. On midsummer eve they had been left to dry undisturbed in room temperature for 24 hours.

## Prep
![Preparing for midnight](https://inkomstkatt.github.io/Diaries-of-a-rave-magician/assets/img/20260619_225655.webp)

My plans for midsummer were initially to be out in the woods camping by a lake, and I was going to do my ritual out there. Alas, this camping trip was not to be and I had to make last-minute backup plans as I still had this pagan duty to attend to. My own forest, a nature reserve near to where I live, would now need to serve as the location. This in a way made things easier, as I am very familiar and used to working the spirits of this forest and the lake it lies by as well. And it is only a short walk from my house, very convenient to get to. I would also be able to work out of my own temple due to this proximity, with all my usual tools and supplies nearby and available.

In preparation I banished and cleansed my temple, then placed the idols on my altar and lit copal incense for them. I also drew a card from the sacred mushroom oracle deck, one of my favourite oracles that never fails to hit just right, and this occasion was no exception: magic. Perfect, that is exactly what we are doing. Let's fucking go.

To prepare myself I put on mystic music and dropped ~125 micrograms of LSD washed down with tea made from 2 grams of syrian rue seeds four hours before midnight. I also partook of a smoking blend of a wide variety of shamanic herbs including cannabis, blue lotus, mugwort, sinicuichi, passionflower and syrian rue, to name some of the more notable psychoactive ingredients. While coming up I went out into the woods to commune with its spirit, having a small private hapé ritual out there and a close friend of mine drum and sing me a galdr via remote link, to set the mood and intentions right.

Returning home for a short stop to retrieve the idols I again banished myself and the temple space, had another ceremony for myself involving more hapé and also sananga (which had very fascinating interactions with the LSD, a topic for another later post to explore more in-depth) in order to really clear my energy field and get into the right psychedelic headspace. Once I felt ready I gathered my supplies in backpack, put on ceremonial music in my earphones, and went out to the woods at 23:30.

## Into the Night
![One last drink with the gods before we part](https://inkomstkatt.github.io/Diaries-of-a-rave-magician/assets/img/20260619_2350362.webp)

I knew already the perfect spot, and luckily it was unoccupied. This spot was a municipal fire place in the reserve. I wasn't going to make a proper fire, but it was near the water and had a rustic wood table and some seats set up. Arriving there with 20 minutes to spare I set up with tea candles and the idols and lit more copal incense. With me I had a bottle of (alcohol-free) mead and a cup, and while we awaited midnight me and the gods had a final farewell drink before it was their time to part. I lit another magick shaman spliff at this point as well, and my consciousness was firmly altered to say the least.

I thanked the gods for the year that has been since last midsummer, complimented them for their virtues and glories, and gave a hearty cheers to them and to the summer itself.

Then, as midnight grew close, as a final act before there ceremony I took out my trusty bag of divination runes and asked the gods to foretell of the coming year.

## What the Gods Foretold
![What the gods foretold of the coming year](https://inkomstkatt.github.io/Diaries-of-a-rave-magician/assets/img/20260619_2356032.webp)

After hapè and meditation to connect with the energies and touch upon a magick gnosis state I drew runes from the bag, intuitively reaching for three rune stones each for the gods, starting with Freya before drawing three for Freyr. I lined them as I drew them and put them on a piece of leather that acted as my divination altar. The photo above shows the stones as I had just drawn them, arranged by god in order of their drawing.

For those who do not have rune meditation memorised (including your's truly), I have summarised the runes and some of their common meanings below.

### Freya’s Divination
1. Ansuz – The rune of the Aesir. Wisdom, communication. Spirit, life force, and inspiration.
2. Tiwaz – Forward and outward flowing energy. Boldness with balance. Masculinity. The warrior rune of Tyr.
3. Ehwaz – The horse rune. Travel and communication between different planes. A shamanic power rune.

### Freyr’s Divination:
1. Sowilo – The life-giving power of the sun. Summer. Warmth and light.
2. Jera – The year's growth. Sowing in order to reap. Darkness meeting the light. Spring. The cycle of life.
3. Aegir – The moose rune. The animal kingdom. Inner strength. Survival instinct. Protection against evil forces.

Intepreting these omens is left as an exercise to the reader, as well as myself as I am not certain what to make out of it as of this writing. Overall the message seems growth-oriented, relating to stepping into power and achieving strength and potential within oneself. This resonates a lot with my own personal journey and the trajectory I have been on lately, and perhaps this divination is best interpreted as a personal one on my part. However, I hope that it rather also relates to the world and "my people", as it were, and that I get to maybe share some of the growth journey I have been on myself with others as well. That is regardless my goal, in any case.

## The Ceremony
![The ceremony at midnight](https://inkomstkatt.github.io/Diaries-of-a-rave-magician/assets/img/20260620_144720.webp)

As the clock struck midnight I propped my phone up and started recording video, then carrying the idols stepped to the water's edge and started the ceremony.

Speaking Swedish with an "old time-y" formal tone I raised the idols to the lake, gave them a silent prayer and final thank you before I first lifted Frey and said the following (translated):

"Oh Freyr, king of summer  
Oh Freyr, you powerful  
Oh Freyr, lord of lords  
Of you I ask  
Fortune and Well-Bringing  
Upon my people, and myself"  

Then I yeeted the idol into the lake and lifted Freya:

"Oh Freya, you beautiful  
Oh Freya, queen of summer  
Of you I ask  
Love and Insight  
Upon my people, and myself"  

And then yeeted the second idol into the lake as well, finishing with a silent prayer before walking away from the lake.

The lines delivered were not as I had originally written them down, but the intention was still carried across.

I returned to my tealights and runes and stayed for a bit meditating, banishing and doing hapé before I eventually packed up and returned home.

Later, I uploaded the video to youtube [here](https://youtu.be/iMVfftDyf-M?si=mLLxnGGD2Hm5wvMS).

### The Teenage Girls

Just as I started my pagan proclamations two teenage girls came walking down the path behind me. They stopped and waited for me to finish, before they continued walking away down the path without saying a word.

It was incredibly funny. What incredible timing they had. Wonder what urban legends I have spawned.

## Lessons?

This experience was a valuable lesson in several regards. Firstly, the act of spending time and effort into crafting something elaborate that is to be destroyed felt very powerful. I was expecting it to, granted. Still, there is potential there I wish to explore further in the future.

Second being able to quickly re-adjust plans and make something out of an unplanned set of circumstances was a welcome challenge that I must say I did a great job at facing.

It is much too early to say whether the magick has had any effect, and given the intent was not framed in a very precise or measurable manner it's going to be very interpretative. Truthfully this was more of a spiritual than a strictly magickal exercise as such. I make no excuses. The way I live my life the distinction is not often clear. And above all it was really fun, however one classifies it. There is also the foretelling, of which the future will have to decide if it speaks true.

It was not the midsummer I had planned or wanted, but it was a valuable and really cool magicky psychedelic pagan experience, and a worthy topic to be the first post on this blog.

Happy midsummer
